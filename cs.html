💡 How to use this page

Craft & Structure is about a quarter of the Reading & Writing section — roughly 13 to 15 questions per test. It comes in three question types, and they all reward the same habit: work out your own answer before you read the choices.

On every question, read the question, decide which type it is, then use the method for that type. Each tab gives you the method, the mistake the test is hoping you make, a quick check to confirm your answer, and a real example.

Open a type from the tabs above or the cards below.

🎯 The Foundation — Predict, then eliminate

Most mistakes on these questions come from one habit: reading the four choices first. Decide what the answer should be, in your own words, before you look at A through D.

✓ What wins
Read for what the text does and what each author claims. Form your own answer first, then find the choice that matches it.
⊖ What loses
Reading for what the text is about (topic) and picking the choice with familiar words in it. Every type plants a trap that does exactly this.
🔮 The universal 3-step
1. Predict — cover the choices, answer in your own words.  2. Name the move — the meaning (Words in Context), the function verb (Text Structure), or the relationship (Cross-Text).  3. Eliminate against the text — cross out anything the passage doesn't actually support.
